<h2><code>EXCEPT</code></h2><p>The operation <code>EXCEPT</code> returns those rows that are in the first <code>SELECT</code> But not in the second. The duplicate rows of the first <code>SELECT</code> will be reduced to one before the comparison begins.</p><h3>Syntax</h3><blockquote><p><code>SELECT query</code></p><p><code>EXCEPT</code></p><p><code>SELECT query</code></p></blockquote><p>The number of columns returning both consultations <code>SELECT</code> must be the same and the types of such columns must be compatible.</p><h3>Example:</h3><p>It shows the employees of department 20 who had not previously been doing another job in the same department:</p><blockquote><p><code>SELECT DISTINCT FIRST_NAME, LAST_NAME<br/>FROM employees<br/>WHERE DEPARTMENT_ID = 20<br/>EXCEPT<br/>SELECT DISTINCT FIRST_NAME, LAST_NAME<br/>FROM job_history JOIN employees USING (EMPLOYEE_ID)<br/>WHERE job_history.DEPARTMENT_ID = 20;</code></p></blockquote><table align="center" border="1" summary="Results from query"><tbody><tr><th style="background-color:#9e9e9e; text-align:center">FIRST_NAME</th><th style="background-color:#9e9e9e; text-align:center">LAST_NAME</th></tr><tr><td>Pat</td><td>Fay</td></tr></tbody></table><h3>Exercise</h3><p><code>Neena Kochhar</code> It is not comfortable in the current department, so it is thinking of changing, not only of department, but also of country.</p><p>You need to find a department that's not in the country whose ID is <code>'US'</code> and in which the average wage is greater than 8000</p><p>Use <code>EXCEPT</code> to help <code>Neena Kochhar</code> to find the department you need</p>
