<h2><code>INTERSECT</code></h2><p>In the operation <code>INTERSECT</code>the ranks of the two are examined <code>SELECT</code> and those that appear in both sets will be returned. The duplicate rows will be removed.</p><h3>Syntax</h3><blockquote><p><code>SELECT query</code></p></blockquote><p><code>INTERSECT</code></p><p><code>SELECT query</code></p><p>The number of columns returning both consultations <code>SELECT</code> must be the same and the types of such columns must be compatible.</p><h3>Example:</h3><p>It shows employees who are doing a job other than they have already developed in the same department:</p><blockquote><p><code>SELECT DISTINCT FIRST_NAME, LAST_NAME, DEPARTMENT_ID<br/>FROM employees<br/>INTERSECT<br/>SELECT DISTINCT FIRST_NAME, LAST_NAME, job_history.DEPARTMENT_ID<br/>FROM job_history JOIN employees USING (EMPLOYEE_ID);</code></p></blockquote><table align="center" border="1" summary="Results from query"><tbody><tr><th style="text-align: center; background-color: rgb(158, 158, 158);">FIRST_NAME</th><th style="text-align: center; background-color: rgb(158, 158, 158);">LAST_NAME</th><th style="text-align: center; background-color: rgb(158, 158, 158);">DEPARTMENT_ID</th></tr><tr><td>Jonathon</td><td>Taylor</td><td>80</td></tr><tr><td>Matthew</td><td>Weiss</td><td>50</td></tr><tr><td>Michael.</td><td>Hartstein</td><td>20</td></tr><tr><td>Payam</td><td>Kaufling</td><td>50</td></tr></tbody></table><h3>Exercise</h3><p><code>Neena Kochhar</code> is not comfortable in the current department, so you are thinking of moving to <code>'UK'</code>.</p><p>You need to find a department that is in the country whose identifier is <code>'UK'</code> and that the average wage is greater than 8000</p><p>Use <code>INTERSECT</code> to help <code>Neena Kochhar</code> to find the department you need</p>
