<p>In the previous years, the data resulting from the execution of the sentences were shown in the order in which they were introduced in the table. If we want the data to appear sorted according to the value of any of its attributes, we will add the clause <code>ORDER BY</code>following the following syntax:</p><blockquote><p><code>SELECT [<em>atributos</em>]<br/>FROM [<em>tabla</em>]<br/>ORDER BY [<em>atributos</em>];</code></p></blockquote><h3>Example:</h3><p>The result of the execution of the following sentence:</p><blockquote><p><code>SELECT * FROM<br/>regions;</code></p></blockquote><p>is:</p><table align="center" border="1px solid" summary="Results from query"><tbody><tr><th>REGION_ID</th><th>REGION_NAME</th></tr><tr><td>1</td><td>Europe</td></tr><tr><td>2</td><td>Americas</td></tr><tr><td>3</td><td>Asia</td></tr><tr><td>4</td><td>Middle East and Africa</td></tr></tbody></table><p>Instead, if we want the result to be displayed ordained by the name of the region (<code>REGION_NAME</code>), we will have to add the clause <code>ORDER BY</code>:</p><blockquote><p><code>SELECT * FROM<br/>regions<br/>ORDER BY REGION_NAME;</code></p></blockquote><table align="center" border="1px solid" summary="Results from query"><tbody><tr><th>REGION_ID</th><th>REGION_NAME</th></tr><tr><td>2</td><td>Americas</td></tr><tr><td>3</td><td>Asia</td></tr><tr><td>1</td><td>Europe</td></tr><tr><td>4</td><td>Middle East and Africa</td></tr></tbody></table><h3>Exercise:</h3><p>Show the name (<code>DEPARTMENT_NAME</code>) of departments (<code>departments</code>) sorted by name</p>
