At the moment, we will only compare these functions to the functions <code>MAX</code> and <code>MIN</code>.</p><p>So much. <code>MAX</code> Like <code>MIN</code> compare values of the same column:</p><blockquote><p><code>SELECT MAX(SALARY), MIN(SALARY)<br/>FROM employees;</code></p></blockquote><table align="center" border="1" summary="Results from query 1"><tbody><tr><th style="text-align: center; background-color: rgb(158, 158, 158);">MAX</th><th style="text-align: center; background-color: rgb(158, 158, 158);">MIN</th></tr><tr><td style="text-align:center">24000.00</td><td style="text-align:center">2100.00</td></tr></tbody></table><p>Instead, functions <code>GREATEST</code> and <code>LEAST</code> compare values in the same row.</p><blockquote><p><code>SELECT GREATEST(MIN_SALARY, MAX_SALARY), LEAST(MIN_SALARY, MAX_SALARY)<br/>FROM jobs<br/>WHERE JOB_TITLE = 'President';</code></p></blockquote><table align="center" border="1" summary="Results from query 1"><tbody><tr><th style="background-color:#9e9e9e; text-align:center">GREATEST</th><th style="background-color:#9e9e9e; text-align:center">LEAST</th></tr><tr><td style="text-align:center">40000</td><td style="text-align:center">200,000</td></tr></tbody></table><h3>Exercise:</h3><p>A minimum commission of 30% (.3) has been established for employees. Calculates the salary of employees whose work is <code>'SA_MAN'</code>, taking into account that the commission to apply to the salary is the highest value between: the established minimum commission (.3) and the value of the attribute <code>COMMISSION_PCT</code>.</p><p>Order the result alphabetically by the last names.</p>
