<p><code>MAX(expression)</code> and<code>MIN(expression)</code> Return the values <strong><em>minimum</em></strong> and <strong><em>maximum</em></strong> of expression. Let us remember that, normally, the expression refers to an attribute.</p><h3>Examples:</h3><p>What is the maximum and minimum wage of scheme employees <code>HR</code>?</p><blockquote><p><code>SELECT MAX(SALARY) AS "MAX SALARY", MIN(SALARY) AS "MIN SALARY"<br/>FROM employees;</code></p></blockquote><table align="center" border="1" summary="Results from query"><tbody><tr><th style="background-color:#9e9e9e; text-align:center">MAX SALARY</th><th style="background-color:#9e9e9e; text-align:center">MIN SALARY</th></tr><tr><td style="text-align:center">24000.00</td><td style="text-align:center">2100.00</td></tr></tbody></table><p>These functions not only serve to compare numerical values, but also dates or strings of characters.</p><p>On which dates were made the first and last contract (<code>HIRE_DATE</code>According to the board employees?</p><blockquote><p><code>SELECT MIN(HIRE_DATE) AS "FIRST AGREEMENT", MAX(HIRE_DATE) AS "LAST AGREEMENT"<br/>FROM employees;</code></p></blockquote><table align="center" border="1" summary="Results from query"><tbody><tr><th style="background-color:#9e9e9e; text-align:center">FIRST AGREEMENT</th><th style="background-color:#9e9e9e; text-align:center">LAST AGREEMENT</th></tr><tr><td style="text-align:center">1987-06-17 00:00:00</td><td style="text-align:center">2000-04-21 00:00:00</td></tr></tbody></table><p>In an alphabetical list of countries, what would be the country that would be shown in the first place and which would occupy the last position according to their names?</p><blockquote><p><code>SELECT MIN(COUNTRY_NAME) AS "FIRST COUNTRY", MAX(COUNTRY_NAME) AS "LAST COUNTRY"<br/>FROM countries;</code></p></blockquote><table align="center" border="1" summary="Results from query"><tbody><tr><th style="background-color:#9e9e9e;
